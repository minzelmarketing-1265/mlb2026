How the prediction models work (and what they're not)

Two heuristic sabermetric models run in parallel — neither is trained on historical outcomes, so without backtesting they can't be calibrated. Treat the numbers as defensible starting points, not verdicts.

Blend model (drives the win-probability bar):

  • Team baseline: season R/G and RA/G from MLB standings.
  • Pitcher quality blend: 0.40 × season ERA + 0.40 × Statcast xERA + 0.20 × last-5-starts ERA; components drop and renormalize when missing.
  • Staff composition: starter weighted ~62% of innings; bullpen ~38% blends team season RA (captures pen quality).
  • Park factor: 3-year multi-year run multiplier (Coors 1.18 → Petco 0.93).
  • Weather adjustment (multiplicative): ±1.5% per 10°F off 70°F; heavy precip dampens; high wind ≥15 mph bumps variance.
  • Win probability: Pythagorean expectation (exp 1.83) on projected scores + 3% home-field bump.

Six-Step model (alternate total, from the YouTube video formula):

  • Adjusted SP ERA: (Season ERA + L5 starts ERA) / 2.
  • Starter runs: (adjERA / 9) × 5.5 IP; Bullpen runs: (bullpenERA / 9) × 3.5 IP (bullpen proxied by team RA/G — StatsAPI doesn't expose bullpen-only stats cheaply).
  • Baseline RA = starter + bullpen runs per side.
  • Offensive multiplier: TeamR/G ÷ 4.4, applied to the OPPOSING staff's baseline.
  • Park factor: multiplied into the combined total.
  • Weather adjustment (additive runs, per the video): wind out 10+ mph +0.5, wind in 10+ mph −0.5, wind 5-10 mph ±0.25; hot 85°F+ +0.25; cold <50°F −0.25. Wind direction is computed from the park's home-plate-to-CF azimuth when available, else treated as crosswind (no adjustment).

Known limitations: no historical backtesting, no handedness platoon (yet), no lineup quality (posted ~2h pre-game), no umpire effects, no betting lines, no injury feed, no true bullpen-only ERA (those would need a backend).
